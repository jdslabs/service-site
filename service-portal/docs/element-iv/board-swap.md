## Overview

This guide walks you through replacing the main circuit board in your Element IV. This procedure may be necessary if you're upgrading your board or replacing a faulty unit.

**What you'll need:**
- Replacement Element IV board
- Phillips head screwdriver
- Clean, static-free workspace

## Step 1: Disconnect Power

<div class="repair-step">
  <div class="repair-step-media">
    <img src="/img/element-iv/el4-board-1.JPG" alt="Disconnect power" />
  </div>
  <div class="repair-step-content">
    <p>Unplug the power cable and any connected audio cables from your Element IV.</p>
    <div class="repair-warning">
      <strong>Warning:</strong> Always disconnect power before opening the enclosure to avoid electrical shock or damage.
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

## Step 2: Remove the Enclosure Screws

<div class="repair-step">
  <div class="repair-step-media">
    <img src="/img/element-iv/el4-board-2.JPG" alt="Remove enclosure screws" />
  </div>
  <div class="repair-step-content">
    <p>Remove the screws securing the top panel of the Element IV enclosure.</p>
    <p>Set the screws aside in a safe place to avoid losing them.</p>
  </div>
</div>

## Step 3: Remove the Top Panel

<div class="repair-step">
  <div class="repair-step-media">
    <img src="/img/element-iv/el4-board-3.JPG" alt="Remove top panel" />
  </div>
  <div class="repair-step-content">
    <p>Carefully lift the top panel away from the enclosure to expose the internal board.</p>
    <div class="repair-info">
      <strong>Tip:</strong> Work on a clean, static-free surface to protect the electronics.
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

## Step 4: Disconnect Internal Cables

<div class="repair-step">
  <div class="repair-step-media">
    <img src="/img/element-iv/el4-board-4.JPG" alt="Disconnect internal cables" />
  </div>
  <div class="repair-step-content">
    <p>Carefully disconnect any ribbon cables or wire harnesses connecting the board to the front panel controls and rear I/O.</p>
    <p>Take note of the cable orientations for reassembly.</p>
  </div>
</div>

## Step 5: Remove the Old Board

<div class="repair-step">
  <div class="repair-step-media"></div>
  <div class="repair-step-content">
    <p>Remove the screws securing the circuit board to the enclosure.</p>
    <p>Carefully lift the old board out of the enclosure.</p>
  </div>
</div>

## Step 6: Install the New Board

<div class="repair-step">
  <div class="repair-step-media"></div>
  <div class="repair-step-content">
    <p>Place the new board into position within the enclosure.</p>
    <p>Secure the board with the mounting screws.</p>
  </div>
</div>

## Step 7: Reconnect Internal Cables

<div class="repair-step">
  <div class="repair-step-media"></div>
  <div class="repair-step-content">
    <p>Reconnect all ribbon cables and wire harnesses to the new board.</p>
    <p>Ensure all connections are secure and properly seated.</p>
  </div>
</div>

## Step 8: Reassemble and Test

<div class="repair-step">
  <div class="repair-step-media"></div>
  <div class="repair-step-content">
    <p>Replace the top panel and secure with the enclosure screws.</p>
    <p>Reconnect power and audio cables, then test the unit to verify proper operation.</p>
    <div class="repair-info">
      <strong>Note:</strong> You may need to update the firmware after installing a new board. See the <a href="/element-iv/firmware-update">Firmware Update</a> guide.
    </div>
  </div>
</div>
